AshDurableWorkflow

A declarative, durable workflow engine for Ash applications. It bridges Ash.Reactor and Oban so your multi-step processes survive application restarts and node crashes — checkpointing to the database after every step and resuming from exactly where they stopped.

REVIEWED THIS ISSUE — A workflow engine that treats "the server died mid-checkout" as a routine event, not a catastrophe. Charge the card once. Ship once. Resume the rest. We put it on the bench.

EVERY developer who has wired up a multi-step background job knows the dread: the process crashes after step three of five, the retry runs the whole thing again, and suddenly a customer's card is charged twice. AshDurableWorkflow exists to make that class of bug structurally impossible.

The premise is simple but powerful. You describe your process as an ordinary Ash.Reactor — a graph of steps with explicit dependencies. AshDurableWorkflow then drives that Reactor one step at a time inside an Oban job. After each step completes, it serializes the entire Reactor state into a dedicated database table. That serialized snapshot is a checkpoint.

If the node dies, the deploy rolls, or the BEAM simply hiccups, Oban retries the job — and the worker finds the last checkpoint, deserializes it, and continues from the next unfinished step. Completed steps are never re-run. The engine calls Reactor.run/4 with max_iterations: 1 and async?: false, looping until the workflow reports {:ok, result}.

The result is a programming model where "exactly-once-on-resume" is the default, retries come with Oban's exponential backoff for free, and the whole apparatus is declared in a few lines of a Spark DSL inside your existing Ash resource.

"It treats a mid-checkout crash as a routine event — not a 2 a.m. incident. Resume from the last checkpoint and move on."

01 On The Bench — Feature Rundown

①

Durable Checkpointing

State is snapshotted with :erlang.term_to_binary/1 after every successful step. Bit-perfect Reactor restoration — no replay, no guesswork.

②

Resume, Don't Restart

An interrupted workflow picks up at its last checkpoint. A worker crash becomes an Oban retry that finds the :halted snapshot and continues.

③

Exactly-Once Steps

Across the full resumed lifecycle, each completed step runs at most once. Pair with idempotency keys to make failed-step retries safe too.

④

Declarative Spark DSL

A clean durable_workflow do … end block on any Ash resource. Map inputs from record attributes or static values, no boilerplate.

⑤

Isolated Storage

Checkpoints live in a library-managed ash_durable_workflow_states table. Your domain tables stay pure.

⑥

Retention Policies

:keep for a full audit trail, :clear_state to drop the heavy blob, :destroy to remove it — applied immediately or deferred via a scheduled job.

⑦

Oban Retries & Backoff

Failures ride Oban's persistent queue with exponential backoff. Tune max_attempts right in the DSL.

⑧

Igniter Installer

One command — mix igniter.install — wires up Oban, the queue, the repo config, and the storage domain. Then codegen the migrations.

02 Hands On — A Checkout Saga

Declaring a workflow takes three pieces: the steps, a Reactor that wires them into a dependency chain, and the resource that makes it durable. Add the extension, point it at your Reactor, map the inputs, and you are done.

The DSL below charges a card, reserves stock, and sends a confirmation. If the box dies after the charge but before the shipment, the retry resumes at :ship — the card is not charged twice, because that step's result is already in the checkpoint.

Kicking it off is a single function call. It enqueues an Oban job; the worker resolves the inputs from the record and begins stepping. Extra inputs passed at call time are merged over the DSL inputs and win.

Crucially, you always go through run_workflow/2 — never Reactor.run/4 directly — so execution stays checkpointed and resumable.

03 Under The Hood — The Checkpoint Loop

The engine's heart is a loop in the Runner. Each iteration runs exactly one step; the executor hands back {:halted, reactor}; that struct is serialized into a WorkflowState row before the next step runs. On success the configured retention policy fires.

Because state is serialized at every boundary, the rule is simple: keep it serializable. Pass references — S3 keys, row ids — between steps, not PIDs, sockets, or giant blobs.

▌Figure 1 — Execution & Checkpoint Flow
run_workflow(record) ──▶ Oban job ──▶ AshDurableWorkflow.Worker
                                            │  find / create WorkflowState
                                            ▼  (isolated table)
                                   AshDurableWorkflow.Runner
        ┌──────────── loop: one step at a time (async? false) ───────────┐
        │  {:halted, reactor} ─▶ term_to_binary ─▶ ✓ checkpoint row       │
        └────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
                                            │
                       {:ok, result} ──▶ apply retention policy
                       {:error, _}    ──▶ Oban retry resumes from checkpoint

⚠ Field Note
A failing step is retried, so guard external side effects with an idempotency key (e.g. "charge:#{order_id}"). Need rollback? Reactor's compensate/4 & undo/4 have you covered.
